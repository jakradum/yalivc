Our investment areas — Fund II thesis
Our Thesis · Fund II — Part 1 of 2
Life Sciences
01
Digital Health Infrastructure
India building a foundational health data layer through ABDM — interoperable records, longitudinal patient data, and a true national health data stack.
02
Volume Cost Arbitrage
Cost advantage combined with large, diverse patient populations allows high-volume data generation and globally relevant, scalable datasets.
03
Specialised Talent Base
Growing interdisciplinary teams across biology, medicine and engineering enable the formation of globally competitive healthtech platforms.
Robotics
01
Quick Commerce as Catalyst
India's 10-minute delivery boom has created a uniquely intense domestic proving ground for AMR systems — giving Indian startups real-world deployment data that most global competitors lack.
02
iDEX as a Springboard
The defence innovation framework acts as patient first-customer for robotics startups building UGVs and surveillance systems, providing non-dilutive grants and de-risking early hardware.
03
Platform, Not Just Product
The most durable value in Indian robotics will be built at the software and middleware layer — AI training pipelines, robot OS, and simulation tools.
Fabless Semiconductor
01
The "Series A" Breakout
For the first time, multiple startups — C2i, Vervesemi, Mindgrove — have crossed the $8M–$15M threshold. VCs are now backing the high-CAPEX tape-out phase.
02
Strategic Sovereignty
Investment in RISC-V (Mindgrove, Morphing Machines) and GaN (AGNIT) reflects a national push to own the IP stack and reduce dependence on Western proprietary architectures.
03
Efficiency over Raw Power
The power bottleneck has become AI infrastructure's #1 constraint — driving a new wave of Indian chip designs optimised for performance-per-watt over raw throughput.
Our Thesis · Fund II — Part 2 of 2
Artificial Intelligence
01
Applied AI, Not Frontier Labs
India's strongest position is enterprise deployment and workflow software. 1,700+ GCCs create natural demand — Uniphore, Emergent and Gupshup show India builds AI products, not just services.
02
Sovereign AI Is Becoming Concrete
IndiaAI Mission's 38,000+ GPUs at ₹65/hour. Neysa's $1.2B Blackstone-led raise is the first large-scale global PE commitment to Indian AI compute.
03
Global from Day One
Qure.ai operates across 90+ countries with FDA clearance; Uniphore serves Fortune 500 globally; Sarvam's Azure partnership gives hyperscaler distribution from launch.
Smart Manufacturing
01
PLI Has Created Real Industrial Pull
$26B in realised investments, $191B in production output. Electronics manufacturing up 146% in four years — creating genuine demand for smart factory infrastructure.
02
India's Right to Win Is Applied Industrial Tech
Advanced manufacturing startups have grown 10x in five years. Indian manufacturing DNA combined with software-first thinking is a natural competitive advantage.
03
The Gap Is Factory Software, Not Hardware
Most factories still run on Excel and manual inspection. The IP opportunity is in MES, predictive maintenance, AI quality, and supply chain intelligence.
Aerospace & Surveillance
01
iDEX as First Customer
Non-dilutive grants up to ₹25 crore and direct procurement pathways — functioning as patient risk capital bridging the Valley of Death for deep-tech hardware. 600+ startups funded since 2018.
02
The ISR-to-DEW Stack
Tonbo (sensing / ISR) and DeepLase (directed energy / photonics) together span the full kill chain — from detecting the threat to neutralising it.
03
Export Optionality
Tonbo's global ISR traction and NewSpace's army procurement contracts signal a credible path from domestic supplier to global defense exporter.
